SECTION 6 — What Drives You
Dimensions scored: Motivational Fit — all four sub-dimensions (Mastery, Impact, Recognition, Autonomy), Learning Velocity  ·  Est. time: 8–10 min

Framing shown at section start
"This section is about what genuinely motivates you — not what sounds impressive, but what actually gives you energy and keeps you engaged over time. The answers here help us understand whether a role is the right fit for you long-term, not just on paper."

Section 6 reflective narrative (required, 60–120 words)
Experienced: "Describe a role or project where you felt genuinely motivated — not just engaged, but actually energised by the work itself. What was it about that situation that produced that feeling? And if you can, describe a contrasting situation where the motivation was harder to find."

Early career: "Describe something you’ve done where you felt genuinely energised. What was it about that situation that produced that feeling?"

LLM rubric for Section 6 narrative
Criterion	What LLM evaluates
Intrinsic vs extrinsic language	Mastery vs Recognition: Does the description reference internal satisfaction or external validation?
People vs task orientation	Impact vs Autonomy: Do they describe motivation through relationships and effect on others, or through independent work and personal achievement?
Consistency check	All four sub-dims: Does the narrative align with the S6Q3 ranking and anchored scale selections, or does it contradict them?
Self-awareness quality	Learning Velocity: Do they show genuine insight into their own motivational pattern or describe it in generic terms?

⛑ The consistency check criterion is particularly valuable. A candidate who ranked Recognition last in S6Q3 but describes their most motivated experience entirely in terms of external validation is flagging a meaningful inconsistency worth surfacing.
